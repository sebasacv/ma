The problem

Mid-market tech diligence is broken

Buyers are flying blind. Sellers are paranoid. And everyone is cobbling together spreadsheets and NDAs.

Buyers can't see the code

Sellers won't open up their codebase to a counterparty. You're making a multi-million dollar bet on a vague tech description and a few engineer interviews.

Big Four is too slow and expensive

A traditional tech diligence engagement costs $80K–$400K and takes 6–8 weeks. Most mid-market deals don't have that kind of budget or timeline.

Self-reported data isn't credible

Asking the seller to fill a questionnaire about their own tech stack is like asking a car dealer to write the inspection report. The incentives are wrong.

Integration surprises kill value

Post-close tech integration is where deals fall apart. Without a real assessment upfront, you're discovering the hard problems after you've already paid.

How it works

We look. You decide.

We act as a trusted third party — we see the code, you get the insight. The seller's IP never leaves our secure environment.

1

Secure access granted

Seller provides read-only access to their code repositories in our isolated, audited environment.

2

Automated scan runs

Our tooling analyzes stack composition, dependencies, vulnerabilities, architecture patterns, and code quality signals.

3

Analysts review findings

Our team reviews raw output, validates signals, and removes any code-level detail before it leaves our environment.

4

Buyer gets the report

You receive a structured, analyst-reviewed assessment — risks rated, integration effort scored, red flags surfaced. No raw code. No IP.

Privacy-preserving by design

The buyer never sees source code, schema details, or architecture diagrams. Auto M&A acts as a neutral intermediary — our findings are our own analysis, not a copy of the seller's IP. Both parties sign our standard tri-party NDA before any access is granted.
